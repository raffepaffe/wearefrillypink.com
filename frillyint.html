An interview with Frilly Pink:
 By Lloyd Ffwylch, Welsh Times

I'm sitting in a small studio in northern Wales with the band that is a model to several young people. The name of the band? Frilly Pink ofcourse!
How do you feel about beeing a rolemodel to young people?

CC: Piss off!
Steve: We have the chance to lead them on to a better life, to give them something. But we dont!
MC: Yeah!
Dave: Except for the girls. We give them a lot!
CC: The fans are all bastards.

How did you react when the press hailed your new album Mano a Mano as THE album of the century?
CC: We told them to write that, or we'd break their legs.
Steve: And they wrote it, so we broke their legs anyway.

Why?
Steve: Because we're totally unpredictable. Like, you didn't know I was gonna do this to you.

Ow! You bastard! Let go of me!
Steve: OK.

Holy Jesus Christ! OK, tell the fans about a typical Frilly day.
CC: We get drunk.
Steve: Then we get out of bed, and go down to the studio.
Dave: We write some autographs on the way, and grab something to eat.
Steve: How many autographs has your mother got now CC?
CC: Shut up you sissy!
MC: Usually there are some kids in the studio when we get there, but CC fixes that.
CC: Right, I hate kids. If they're not careful they could get ... lost.

What do you do in the studio? How do you get the inspiration for all those hits you've written?
Steve: We jam.
MC: Yupp.
Dave: And party.
Steve: But mostly we just hang around in bars and write songs about people who bother us.

So, basically you just go down to the pub, and those wonderful songs come out?
CC: Uh-hu.

OK, tell me a little about your latest tour to Germany.
MC: It was brilliant! We played at this place in Berlin, and there was, like, almost a hundred people there!

By a hundred, I suppose you mean a hundred thousand?
MC: No, a hundred people. All dressed in black and screaming and runing around like totally crazy!

Oh, you played one of Berlins famous underground arenas then?
Steve: Nope. It was a wedding we gatecrashed.

You are famous for your exciting stage performances. Tell me how you got the idea for the last show here in Wales. The one where CC threw a knocked out policeman into the crowd. That was a brilliant stunt, and really showed how you denounce the whole British legal system.
CC: Bastard's gonna hit me, right, so I had to defend myself, OK?
MC: Pigs.

OK, is there anything else you'd like to tell your fans?
CC: Sod off!
Dave: Girls, you have my number!
MC: The new single is out soon! Buy it!
Steve: Rock on!

Thank you.
